About Me

I'm a PhD Students in Systems neuroscience at the Sainsbury Wellcome Centre - in the Branco Lab. I trained as a biologist in Milano and did a master and research internship in the Duguid lab in Edinburgh.

I started my PhD with an experimental project aimed at investigating the behavioural bases of decision making under threat. More recently, my focus has moved towards a more computational/theoretical perspective: my current interests revolve around ideas related to the computation through dynamics framework. I'm interested in the study of population dynamics and how they related to computations performed by neural circuits.

In my current project I'm investigating the role of several brain regions in the control of goal directed locomotion using multi-units recordings in freely moving animals and RNNs.

Alongside my research projects, I was lucky enough to get involved in several open source projects aimed at providing software which can be freely used by the research community.

scidraw.io


Link: scidraw.io


Scidraw.io is a free open source repository of high quality drawings of animals, scientific setups and everything that might be useful for generating illutrations for presentations and papers.

Content hosted on scidraw.io is generated by individual scientists and is made freely available for anyone to use.

I've created scidraw.io together with Alex Harston.

brainrender


Link: docs.brainrender.info


Brainrender is an open-source Python software for interactive 3D visualization of neuroanatomical datasets.

Brainrender makes it easy to visualize in 3D anatomical data from publicly available datasets and generated within individual laboratories explore the intricated 3D structure of individual neurons and brain regions. By facilitating the cration of high quality renderings, images and interactive visualizations Brainrender also aims to improve how anatomical data are disseminated by the research community.

Brainrender is part of the BrainGlobe suite of python software for computational neuroanatomy.
You can read about Brainrender here.

brainglobe


Link: docs.brainglobe.info


The core goal of BrainGlobe is to develop a suite of Python-based software tools for computational neuroanatomy. We developed several software packages to access, analyze and visualize anatomical data, by ensuring the interoperability of all of BrainGlobe's software we aim to facilitate the development of analysis pipelines and facilitate the process of going from raw data to publication-ready content

BrainGlobe was developed with Adam Tyson and Luigi Petrucco.
You can read about BrainGlobe here.

refy


Link: GitHub repository


Refy is a scientific papers recomendation tool that facilitates finding new, relevant papers for your research.

To find relevant papers refy uses a `.bib` file with metadata about other papers that you've read and found relevant in the past. Unlike other paper recomendation systems it doesn't use single papers or keywords as reference and is thus poised to give more insightful recomendations.

Refy has different recomendation modes, including Daily which recomends relevant preprints that came out in the lat 24 hours on BiorXiv. You can see an exmaple of the papers that refy reccomended for me today here.
